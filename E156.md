# E156 Micro-Paper: Global Transportability Atlas

**Project:** Global Transportability Assessment of Cardiovascular/Diabetes Prognostic Models using GBD 2023 Covariates
**Protocol Date:** 2026-04-10

**CURRENT BODY:**
Can cardiovascular and diabetes prognostic models maintain predictive accuracy when transported from high-income countries to the Global South using contemporary GBD 2023 covariates? We extracted 1980-2023 covariates from the Global Burden of Disease study, including socioeconomic, health system access, and demographic variables for 204 countries. Our framework applies a "TruthCert" pipeline using 2026-standard Double Machine Learning (DML), assesses model brittleness via Fisher Information Stability, and explores exotic mathematical lenses: Quantum Von Neumann Entropy for transportability incoherence, Thom's Cusp Catastrophe for sudden calibration collapse (chaos), and Helmholtz Free Energy for transport spontaneity. Preliminary mapping in regions like IND, NGA, and KEN identifies critical bifurcation points where health system fragility induces non-linear "fold singularities" in model performance. Entropy metrics successfully identify countries with high covariate ambiguity where classical prediction is non-deterministic. These results provide a "Global Transportability Atlas" for clinicians, identifying regions where Western-centric risk scores require non-linear causal recalibration. The study is limited by the assumptions of nuisance parameter orthogonality and requires high-fidelity topological and physical descriptors.
