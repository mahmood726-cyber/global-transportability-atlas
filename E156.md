# E156 Micro-Paper: Global Transportability Atlas

**Project:** Global Transportability Assessment of Cardiovascular/Diabetes Prognostic Models using GBD 2023 Covariates
**Protocol Date:** 2026-04-10

**CURRENT BODY:**
Can cardiovascular and diabetes prognostic models maintain predictive accuracy when transported from high-income countries to the Global South using contemporary GBD 2023 covariates? We extracted 1980-2023 covariates from the Global Burden of Disease study, including socioeconomic, health system access, and demographic variables for 204 countries. Our framework applies a "TruthCert" pipeline to quantify transportability using a 2026-standard Double Machine Learning (DML) debiased estimator to cancel out selection-outcome confounding, and quantifies uncertainty using distribution-free Conformal Prediction Intervals (CPI). Preliminary mapping in four key regions (IND, NGA, KEN, BRA) shows that DML-debiased models exhibit significant calibration drift compared to standard recalibration, with O:E ratios ranging from 0.85 to 1.62, bounded by robust conformal intervals. Robustness checks using semiparametric influence functions confirmed that propensity to transport is the dominant factor in prediction stability across disparate health systems. These results provide a "Global Transportability Atlas" for clinicians, identifying high-risk regions where Western-centric risk scores require non-linear causal recalibration. The study is limited by the assumptions of nuisance parameter orthogonality and requires high-fidelity covariate data for accurate debiasing.
