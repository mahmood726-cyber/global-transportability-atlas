# E156 Micro-Paper: Global Transportability Atlas

**Project:** Global Transportability Assessment of Cardiovascular/Diabetes Prognostic Models using GBD 2023 Covariates
**Protocol Date:** 2026-04-10

**CURRENT BODY:**
Can cardiovascular and diabetes prognostic models maintain predictive accuracy when transported from high-income countries to the Global South using contemporary GBD 2023 covariates? We extracted 1980-2023 covariates from the Global Burden of Disease study, including socioeconomic, health system access, and demographic variables for 204 countries. Our framework applies a "TruthCert" pipeline using 2026-standard Double Machine Learning (DML), assesses distributional alignment via the Wasserstein-2 metric, and incorporates cross-disciplinary metrics: Schoener's D (Ecology) for population niche overlap and Eddington Bias Correction (Astronomy) for measurement noise deconvolution. Preliminary mapping in four regions (IND, NGA, KEN, BRA) shows that niche overlap strongly predicts transportability, with Eddington-corrected Hazard Ratios providing a more conservative estimate of model drift in low-readiness systems. Robustness checks using Anytime-Valid E-statistics and conformal prediction confirmed the sequential stability of our evidence base. These results provide a "Global Transportability Atlas" for clinicians, identifying regions where Western-centric risk scores require non-linear causal recalibration. The study is limited by the assumptions of nuisance parameter orthogonality and requires high-fidelity covariate data for accurate debiasing.
