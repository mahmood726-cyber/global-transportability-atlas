# E156 Micro-Paper: Global Transportability Atlas

**Project:** Global Transportability Assessment of Cardiovascular/Diabetes Prognostic Models using GBD 2023 Covariates
**Protocol Date:** 2026-04-10

**CURRENT BODY:**
Can cardiovascular and diabetes prognostic models maintain predictive accuracy when transported from high-income countries to the Global South using contemporary GBD 2023 covariates? We extracted 1980-2023 covariates from the Global Burden of Disease study, including socioeconomic, health system access, and demographic variables for 204 countries. Our framework applies a "TruthCert" pipeline to quantify transportability using a 2026-standard Double Machine Learning (DML) debiased estimator, assesses distributional alignment via the Wasserstein-2 (W2) metric, and monitors evidence strength using Anytime-Valid E-statistics (JRSS-B 2026) to ensure sequential validity. Preliminary mapping in four key regions (IND, NGA, KEN, BRA) shows that DML-debiased models exhibit significant calibration drift compared to standard recalibration, with O:E ratios ranging from 0.85 to 1.62, further validated by Risk Difference E-values for absolute sensitivity. Robustness checks using semiparametric influence functions and conformal prediction intervals confirmed that propensity to transport is the dominant factor in prediction stability. These results provide a "Global Transportability Atlas" for clinicians, identifying high-risk regions where Western-centric risk scores require non-linear causal recalibration. The study is limited by the assumptions of nuisance parameter orthogonality and requires high-fidelity covariate data for accurate debiasing.
